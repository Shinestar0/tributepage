Steve Jobs

1955-2011

"Design is not just with it looks like and feels like . Design is how it works"

Biography

Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple Inc., is a man who has made significant contributions to technology and business. He is best known for developing the iPhone, which revolutionized the way people use mobile devices. Jobs is also credited with developing the World Wide Web and contributing to the development of the internet as we know it today. Jobs is also known for his philanthropic work, which includes advocating for climate change and education. He has also been involved in numerous other causes, such as animal rights and environmental conservation.

Education

Steve Jobs attended Stanford University and later earned a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. He later pursued a Ph.D. in Computer Science at MIT. He also studied at Harvard Business School and MIT Sloan School of Management.

Career

Steve Jobs was a founding CEO of Apple Inc. He led the company from its inception in 1976 to its present day. He was also a founding member of the Apple Computer Science Club and a member of the Apple Alumni Association. Jobs was also a member of the National Academy of Engineering. He is also a member of the National Endowment for the Arts and Sciences.

Awards

Steve Jobs has received numerous awards, including the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 1988, the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 1991, the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 1996, the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 2001, the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 2005, and the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 2010. He has also been awarded the Apple Computer Award for Best Innovation in 2011.
